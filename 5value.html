Coraline

Coraline is an important gothic animated adaptation based on another gothic novel by Neil Gailmam. Gothic narratives are important in children's literature.

  • "Gothicism has always been an integral part of children't literature... To come into contact with monsters and ghosts is vital and necessary to our mental and physical growth because as we grow older we discover that these strange shapes and intense situations often embody our individual fears and anxieties."
  • "Gothicism is just as applicable to children as it is to adults. There is no emotion specific to adults and... children and adults possess the same fears, but those fears take on different forms depending on one's age and maturity."
  • "Subconsciously, childresn may perceive a story's gothic tone, recognize the emotions manufactures by the mood or characters or setting, and begin to cogitate on why they are responding to the literature."
  • Gothic conetent within both the book and the film contributes to undermining the idealization of Coraline's family, her own process of growing up, and he coping with moving to a completely different place."
  • Coraline teaches children and adults of all genders about courage, perspective, and gratitude.

Turning Red

Turning Red centers on a family going through acceptance and change.

  • Complex mother and daughter relationship and healing
  • Cultural difference in parental styles (especially immigrant families) and the lack of personal space and privacy.
  • How the self-effacement depicted is a direct result of a lack of boundaries, and how it ends up perpetuating a familiar cycle of intergenerational trauma.
  • The importance of a support system/ authentic depiction of friendship formed in adolecence.
  • Depicts the protagonist creating a space for both her cultural familiar traditions as well as the dominant western world where she lives.

I'm No Longer Here

I'm No Longer Here tells a story of life in Monterrey, Mexico and immigration.

  • Cultural perspective on Mexican diaspora and the identity crisis, and expected change/ assimilation that comes with it.
  • Experiencing neglect, rejection, and unauthentic love through themes of cultural appropriation.
  • The reality that some people do not get happy endings; challenges films to offer real stories that break traditional 3-act structures. Protagonist does not have a happy ending and experiences no personal growth. We only experience his experience and that is important, as it helps a wider audience understand the hardships of living in Mexico and why so many cross the border.
