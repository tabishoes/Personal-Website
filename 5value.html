The earliest categorical movie is actually not a movie at all. Eadweard Muybridge, a pioneer photographer, was hired in 1878 to settle a controversy of whether or not all the feet of a trotting horse were clear of the ground in the same instant.

For this experiment, he constructed a track and set up multiple cameras connected to a wire, each stretched across the track. As the race horse ran, each camera was triggered to capture an instant in time.

In the end, the series of photos proved that indeed, the feet of a trotting horse were clear of the ground in the same instant, but only when they were under the horse's body.

Following the photographs, in 1880 The Horse in Motion was made by compiling the photos and projecting it onto a zoopraxiscope disc, creating the illusion of a moving horse.
