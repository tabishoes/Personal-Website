My Favorites

Movies matter.

They teach us lessons and are reliable comforts in our time of need.

They set examples of healthy relationships, show the journey of life obstacles, help define our values and virtues, bridge different experiences and perspectives, teach us how to be brave and kind, warn us of how our actions can shape our society, allow room for needed catharsis, and so much more.

La La Land

La La Land is a movie about chasing your dreams, and the sacrifices one must make to get there.

Some of the tools used to create the story is the use of color and light.

In the beginning, when Mia is actively chasing her dreams, her wardrobe is bright and colorful, and usually contrasted by a washed out background. Towards the end, as she gives up her hopes, her colors become lifeless.

Another tool used is the direction of camera movement. The camera is lively and fast paced during the scene where Mia and Sebastian are in the jazz club.

The rapid camera panning drives a sense of excitement and joy as the two play a call-and-response game of sorts.

Rahter than experiencing this as an observer, we feel like a participant, creating a bond between us and the characters that will be vital to create empathy and hard-hitting moments throughout the story.

Roma

Roma is a movie about the overwhelmingly simplicity of reality. It shows what is't like to be a woman in 1970's Mexico. It is a film that feels personal, real, and shatters the thin line between fiction and documentary- and in a good way. Roma is based on the nanny from the director's childhood who raised him. It focuses on relationships, especially the ones of two different woman who go through very similar experiences. It is a story about family and love, change and loss through a film that has a slower pace, and an appriciation for a culturally different lifestyle.
